There are mainly five Rivers in Punjab :


  1. Sutlej
  2. Ravi
  3. Beas
  4. Chenab

  • Sutlej

    The Sutlej River is one of the five rivers that flow through Punjab, India. It is the longest of the five rivers, with a length of approximately 1,500 kilometers. The river originates from Lake Rakshastal in Tibet, and flows through Himachal Pradesh and Punjab before merging with the Chenab River in Pakistan. The Sutlej River is an important source of water for irrigation and hydroelectric power generation in Punjab. The Sutlej River has played a significant role in the history and culture of Punjab. It was a major transportation route for trade in ancient times, and has been mentioned in various Hindu and Buddhist texts. The river has also been the site of several battles throughout history, including the battles of Mudki and Ferozeshah during the First Anglo-Sikh War in 1845.

  • Ravi

    The Ravi River is a transboundary river that flows through northwestern India and eastern Pakistan. It is one of the five rivers of Punjab and has played a significant role in the history, culture, and economy of the region. The river originates in the Himalayas in Himachal Pradesh, India and flows through the Indian state of Punjab before entering Pakistan, where it eventually merges with the Chenab River. The Ravi River was once a major tributary of the Indus River, but changes in its course have resulted in it flowing into the Chenab instead. The river is an important source of water for irrigation and hydroelectric power generation in both India and Pakistan. The Ravi River basin is also home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including a number of endangered species such as the Indus dolphin. The Ravi River has been a significant part of the history and culture of the region. It has been mentioned in various ancient texts and literature, and has been the site of several battles throughout history, including the Battle of Ravi in 1841 during the Anglo-Sikh Wars.
